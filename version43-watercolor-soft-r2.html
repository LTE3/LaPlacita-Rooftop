our little corner
est. above brooklyn

A rooftop, painted in citrus light.

La Placita is our small island in Brooklyn — a rooftop kitchen and bar where the recipes come from Bayamón abuelas, the cocktails taste like ripe guava, and the music is always a little too loud after sunset.

We pull from the Caribbean, from family kitchens, from the corner colmados of San Juan, and from the messy, beautiful neighborhood right below us. Come hungry, stay through the music.

— the Placita family

la barra
bar program

Cocktails like summer in a jar.

Our bar is built on rum, citrus, and the kind of fruit that stains your fingers. House syrups, fresh pressed juice, smoked salt rims — nothing pre-bottled, everything painted by hand.

la terraza
the open air

Where the city turns soft.

Lanterns on the rails, the smell of plantain hitting butter, conversation in two languages and three keys. Our terrace was built for the long hours between sunset and last call.

Private parties, weddings, birthdays, the random Wednesday. We hold the whole roof or just a corner of it.
