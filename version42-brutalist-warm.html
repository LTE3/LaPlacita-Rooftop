Chapter / About

A loud, sun-baked corner of San Juan, parked on a Brooklyn roof.

La Placita is a Latin and Puerto Rican rooftop restaurant and bar set high above the borough. We pour rum like it owes us money, grill plantains over open coals, and let the trade winds do the talking once the sun drops.

Concrete floors. Clay-tiled bar. String lights. Bachata until close. Bring an appetite, leave with a sunburn and a story.

"Tropical, terracotta, and a little bit feral."

02 The Bar Program

Rum, mezcal, & sun-stained citrus.

Tropical cocktails built on Caribbean and Mexican spirits — guava daiquiris, pineapple-mezcal highballs, a tamarind sour you'll think about for weeks.
