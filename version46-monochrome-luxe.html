The House

A small terrace
that remembers
the island.

La Placita is a slow room held above the borough — warm lamplight, ceramic bowls, the hush of a record turning. We cook the food we grew up on, plated with care: lechon glazed dark and patient, sofrito built each morning, plantains crackled to lace.

Our bar program is single-minded in the best way. Rum done right. A handful of clarified classics. A short, considered list of Caribbean wine. Nothing here is loud. Everything is intentional.

From the kitchen · Chef R. Vega
The Bar

Rum, slow.
Citrus, cold.
Conversation, longer.

Twelve cocktails. Aged Puerto Rican rum, house tinctures pressed from hibiscus and tamarind, mezcales kept under the bar. The list shifts with the season — the temperament does not.

A simple idea,
quietly upheld.

Three things we hold above all else. They are not innovations — they are habits, refined nightly, the way a house should be kept.

01 ·

Hand-stirred bar

Every drink built one at a time. No batch, no shortcut. The rum gets the attention it has earned.

02 ·

Short, seasonal menu

Twelve plates at a time. The kitchen cooks what it has chosen well, and lets the rest go.

03 ·

One open rooftop

Forty seats under the Brooklyn sky. Heaters when it’s cold. Blankets if you ask. The room stays calm.

The Terrace

Forty seats.
One sky.

Twelve tables, a long bar against the rail, and the cityline behind it. Heated for winter. Curtained from the wind. Private bookings welcome after ten — for the room, or for the whole roof.
