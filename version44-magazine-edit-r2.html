II. Chapter Two · Origin & Intent
The House — 12 — Feature
Standfirst

A short essay on how a Bushwick rooftop learned to keep island time — the music, the rum, the abuelas, the cumbia at 1 a.m.

Filed under

Latin · Caribbean · Puerto Rican · Brooklyn · Rooftop · Late Night

A Plaza in the Sky, & the slow art of throwing a party that lasts until sunrise.

Long before the elevator opens onto the terrace, you can hear it — bomba on the speakers, ice clattering into shakers, somebody laughing too loud at the bar. La Placita is named for the open-air plazas of San Juan and Mayagüez, the kind where neighbours gather without an agenda. We built ours six storeys up, in the middle of Bushwick, and let the city handle the skyline.

The kitchen leans Puerto Rican with detours through Cuba, the DR, and Colombia. The bar is rum-forward and unapologetically loud. The crowd is mixed, the dress code is whatever the weather says, and the music — salsa, perreo, boleros at the end — runs from sundown to last call.

What you’ll find on the following pages: a rum-led drinks programme, a terrace built for golden hour, a calendar that takes Sunday seriously, and a printed table of hours. Welcome to the Issue.
