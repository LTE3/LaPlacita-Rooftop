Feature · Brooklyn, NY · Estd. 2024

It begins, as the best things do, at golden hour.

The elevator opens to terracotta tile and the smell of charred plantain. The skyline arrives all at once — the East River bending east, the Manhattan grid blinking awake. Twelve stories below, a delivery truck. Up here, a steel drum on a vinyl record.

La Placita Rooftop is the kind of place you didn't know Brooklyn was missing — a sliver of San Juan in the clouds, a block party in the sky, a rum bar where the bartender knows the difference between Don Q Cristal and the small-batch stuff in the back.

PhotographThe terrace at sunset — string lights, terracotta tile, twelve stories above the borough.

— Chapter Two

The bar, like the island, runs on rum.

Aged rum, white rum, spiced rum, the small-batch bottles smuggled back from Old San Juan in a bartender's suitcase. Every cocktail is a postcard — a mango-pasion margarita; a smoked-coconut old fashioned; a coquito that drinks like dessert.

Non-drinkers get the same love. The zero-proof menu is just as fearless — fresh-pressed pineapple, hibiscus shrubs, coconut horchata over hand-cut ice.

Photograph · Plate IIIThe terrace from above, twelve stories over the borough — a sliver of San Juan caught between river and skyline.
The best rooftop in Brooklyn isn't trying to be the loudest. It's just trying to be the warmest.
— A Note · From the Editor
— Chapter Three

Hand-painted murals. Vinyl plena. Open arms.

The space was painted by Boricua artists from across the borough. The records were curated by a DJ from Bayamón. The terracotta tile was shipped from Ponce in three pallets and laid by hand over a single Brooklyn weekend.

By day, brunch under the umbrellas. By night, the skyline becomes the wallpaper and the sound becomes the salsa.
