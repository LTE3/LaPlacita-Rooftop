Our Story

A Taste of
the Island, Above
the City

Step out of the elevator and the city falls away. Salt-warm air, the slow strum of cuatro strings, glasses clinking under string lights — for a moment, Brooklyn becomes the cobbled plaza of Old San Juan at golden hour.

La Placita was born from a simple longing: to bottle a Puerto Rican summer evening and serve it on a rooftop above New York. Every dish is a memory from abuela’s kitchen, every cocktail a sunset over Vieques. We are not a restaurant pretending to be the island — we are the island, transplanted, alive, and waiting for you.

Meet the Chef

Chef Rafael “Rafi” Santiago

Executive Chef · Born in Loiza, raised in Brooklyn

Rafi grew up between two kitchens — his abuela’s in Loiza, where the sofrito always hit before the front door, and his mother’s in Bushwick, where she stretched island recipes across a Brooklyn winter. After fifteen years cooking through Madrid, San Juan, and Manhattan’s finest Latin kitchens, he came home to build the menu he’d been writing in his head since he was a kid.

“Every plate at La Placita is a postcard,” he says. “I want Brooklyn to taste what August feels like in Vieques.”

— Rafi
