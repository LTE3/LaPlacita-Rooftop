A NOTE FROM THE PRINTERS

Two colors,
one island,
endless heat.

La Placita started as a flier taped to a lamppost on Knickerbocker Ave — an invitation to a backyard, a pot of arroz con gandules, and a speaker pointed at the sky. We grew up. The pot got bigger. The speaker became a rooftop.

What didn't change is the press. Every night we run a fresh edition: handful of cocktails pulled from the bar's rum library, a kitchen that argues with itself in Spanish, a playlist that takes you from Hector Lavoe to Bad Bunny without flinching. Come read the poster.

Side A · The Bar

Cold glass,
hot island.

Our cocktail program is a love letter mailed from Old San Juan to Bushwick. Aged rums from Ponce, mezcals from Oaxaca, fresh-pressed cane juice, citrus that arrives every morning at six. Bartenders who pour like printmakers — heavy ink, no apology.

★ THREE NIGHTLY ACTS · ONE STAGE ★
01
★ HEADLINER · THE ROOFTOP

Open-air
terrace.

Sixty seats under the Brooklyn sky, string lights strung like staff lines. Bushwick water towers to the north, Manhattan skyline as the encore. Sunsets begin at 7:42 PM and the bar gets louder from there.

SET TIMES · 4–11 PM↗
02
★ MAIN STAGE · THE KITCHEN

Pernil,
tostones,
mofongo.

Our chef grew up between Santurce and the Bronx and the menu argues with itself accordingly. Slow-roasted pork shoulder, smashed plantains, the kind of rice with the crisp bottom you fight your cousin for.

KITCHEN · TIL 11 PM↗
03
★ LATE SHOW · THE SOUND

Live son.
DJ sets.
Loud.

Thursday nights: a live four-piece son cubano outfit. Fridays: DJ Agua spinning salsa dura into reggaeton. Saturdays: open-vinyl. Sundays: nothing but Héctor Lavoe and the smell of pernil. The poster changes weekly.

Side B · The View

One rooftop
over Bushwick.

Six stories up, three blocks from the Jefferson L. The terrace is wood, brick, and a horizon line that disagrees with itself: water towers stage right, the One World Trade postage stamp stage left, a sliver of Queens in the wings. Bring a denim jacket after 10.
