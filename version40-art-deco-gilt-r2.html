The Establishment

Brooklyn Meets the Island

High above the avenue, behind a velvet curtain and a flight of brass stairs, La Placita Rooftop opens onto a terrace of gold leaf, terracotta and palm.

Our kitchen draws from San Juan and the southern shore: lechón crisped over open flame, sofrito stirred patient and slow, plantains gilded to a Tiffany sheen. The bar mixes Caribbean rum with the cold precision of a Manhattan cocktail room.

It is theater. It is tropic. It is Brooklyn, dressed in 24-karat sunset.

"A rooftop where the islands keep company with Park Avenue."

Of Note

Three Pleasures of the Terrace

I.

The Sky Bar

A marble counter under twelve hand-blown lanterns. Bartenders shake to a brass quartet on Thursdays through Sunday.

II.

Lechón Sundays

Whole-roast pig carved tableside, plantain mofongo, and trio of salsas. Reservation required, ceremony optional.

III.

Private Cabanas

Six gilded cabanas with curtains, bottle service and a private view of the Manhattan skyline. Reserved by host.

Above the Avenue

The Terrace, by Lantern Light

When the sun lowers itself behind the bridges and the city begins to shimmer, our terrace becomes the prettiest room in Brooklyn.
